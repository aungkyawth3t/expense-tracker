My Summer Adventure in the Mountains

Last weekend I went on an amazing hiking trip to the Blue Ridge Mountains. The weather was perfect - not too hot, with a cool breeze that made the climb enjoyable. We started early in the morning to catch the sunrise from the summit, and it was absolutely breathtaking!

The trail was challenging but rewarding, with several scenic overlooks along the way. We packed a picnic lunch and enjoyed it at one of the viewpoints. The wildflowers were in full bloom, painting the landscape with vibrant colors.

I highly recommend this hike to anyone looking for a moderate challenge with incredible views. Remember to bring plenty of water and wear good hiking shoes!

My Favorite Vegan Recipes for Summer

With summer officially here, I wanted to share some of my go-to vegan recipes that are perfect for hot days. These dishes are light, refreshing, and packed with flavor!

1. Watermelon Feta Salad

This surprising combination is a game-changer. The sweetness of the watermelon pairs perfectly with the salty feta (I use a vegan version made from almonds). Add some fresh mint and a drizzle of balsamic glaze for a perfect summer side dish.

2. Zucchini Noodles with Pesto

When it's too hot to cook, spiralized zucchini makes the perfect pasta substitute. I make a quick basil pesto with walnuts, garlic, olive oil, and lemon juice. Top with cherry tomatoes for extra freshness.

What are your favorite summer recipes? I'm always looking for new ideas to try!

My Journey to Becoming Vegetarian

Three years ago, I made the decision to become vegetarian. It wasn't an easy transition at first, but now I can confidently say it's one of the best decisions I've ever made for my health and the planet.

In this post, I want to share my top tips for anyone considering making the switch:

  1. Start gradually - maybe with Meatless Mondays
  2. Find good protein alternatives you enjoy
  3. Experiment with international cuisines that are naturally vegetarian-friendly
  4. Don't be too hard on yourself if you slip up

The most surprising benefit? I discovered so many new foods and flavors I never would have tried otherwise!

The Ultimate Guide to Solo Travel

After 5 years of traveling solo across 30 countries, I've learned a thing or two about making the most of traveling alone. Here are my top tips for anyone considering solo travel:

1. Stay in hostels to meet other travelers - even if you're introverted, it's worth stepping out of your comfort zone.

2. Trust your instincts - if a situation feels wrong, remove yourself from it.

3. Pack light - you'll be carrying everything yourself, so only bring essentials.

4. Learn basic phrases in the local language - it goes a long way.

5. Embrace the freedom - you can change your plans on a whim when traveling alone!
